Synopsis

An origin story set in 1981, Joker follows failed comedian Arthur Fleck who turns to a life of crime in Gotham City. Forever alone in a crowd, he seeks connection by walking the streets of Gotham City. Arthur wears two masks – the one he paints for his day job as a clown, and the guise he projects in a futile attempt to feel like he's part of the world around him. Isolated, bullied and disregarded by society, Fleck begins a slow descent into madness as he transforms into the criminal mastermind known as the Joker.

Director Todd Phillips conceived Joker in 2016 and wrote the script with Scott Silver throughout 2017. They were inspired by 1970s character studies, the films of Martin Scorsese, and the premise of the graphic novel Batman: The Killing Joke (1988), which depicted the Joker as a failed stand-up comedian. Since there was no definitive origin story for the Joker in the comics, Phillips and Silver were free to create the story of becoming Joker. While Warner Bros. pushed for Leonardo DiCaprio to be cast as the Joker, Phillips and Silver wrote the script with Phoenix in mind. He was cast in July 2018 and the rest of the cast signed on by August.

Joker premiered at the 76th Venice International Film Festival and won the Golden Lion (Leone d’Oro), one of the film industry’s most prestigious and distinguished prizes. While Joaquin Phoenix’s performance received high acclaim, the portrayal of mental illness and its effects, the handling of violence, and its overall dark tone divided critics.
